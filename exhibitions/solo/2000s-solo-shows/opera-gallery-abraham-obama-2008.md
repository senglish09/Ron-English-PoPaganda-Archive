## Overview

On October 2, 2008, the gallery hosted an event centered on the release of Ron English’s “Abraham Obama” painting — a fusion portrait combining features of Abraham Lincoln and Barack Obama. The original canvas was offered for sale, along with a set of unique colorway variants, each hand-finished by the artist.:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The release attracted interest as part of the cultural conversation around the 2008 U.S. presidential election. The “Abraham Obama” image quickly spread beyond the gallery through prints, reproductions, and media coverage, helping raise English’s profile internationally as a key figure bridging street art, political commentary, and pop-icon imagery.:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
